Section IV

Research Methodology

The following steps were taken to arrive at this analysis. All data was collected and cross-referenced in March 2026.

Internal Compensation Data

Mr. Forbes's current and approved compensation was retrieved directly from the Balentine budget application database. The query targeted the budgetapp_headcount_positions table in the project_management_db PostgreSQL instance, extracting current salary, approved 2026 salary, annual bonus, total compensation, hire date, and location.

Skill Set Documentation

A comprehensive job description was compiled by analyzing twelve months of Mr. Forbes's Slack messages and Outlook emails (both sent and received), cross-referenced with the firm's production codebase and deployed applications. Five parallel research agents scanned communications to identify the full scope of responsibilities, projects, and cross-functional collaboration.

Role Decomposition

Mr. Forbes's responsibilities were decomposed into six distinct professional domains: Full-Stack Engineering, Salesforce Platform Engineering, AI Strategy, Cybersecurity and Compliance, Vendor and Integration Management, and IT Infrastructure and Operations. Each domain was validated against documented evidence from email, Slack, and production systems.

Market Rate Research

Live 2026 salary data was collected from seven independent compensation databases for each of the six role domains, filtered by location (Atlanta, Georgia), industry (Financial Services / Wealth Management), experience level (Senior / Director), and company size (under 100 employees where available).

Comparable Title Analysis

Because Mr. Forbes's role spans responsibilities typically held by three to four separate professionals, the analysis also benchmarked against blended senior titles — VP of Technology and CTO at small firms — to provide a holistic market comparison rather than relying solely on the "Director of IT" title, which significantly understates the role's scope.

Key Factors

1. The title understates the role. "Director of IT" in the market describes someone who manages infrastructure, vendors, and a team but does not write code. Mr. Forbes does all of that and simultaneously serves as the firm's sole software engineer, Salesforce developer, AI strategist, and cybersecurity lead. The title does not reflect the scope of responsibilities documented in this report.

2. The base salary is below market for the title alone. At $160,000, Mr. Forbes's approved 2026 base falls below the average market rate for a standard IT Director in Atlanta ($200,535) — a role that carries none of the engineering, Salesforce, AI, or security responsibilities he performs.

3. The financial services premium is not reflected. Technology roles at SEC-registered investment advisors with $7.5B in assets under advisement typically command 15–25% above general market rates. Mr. Forbes's compensation does not reflect this industry premium.

4. AI leadership adds unquantified strategic value. Mr. Forbes's AI initiatives have earned direct recognition from the CEO and CIO and are actively differentiating the firm in client engagement and operational efficiency. This strategic contribution is not captured in standard salary benchmarks for technology roles.

5. Replacement risk is acute. A single departure would require three to four separate hires at a combined cost of approximately $715,000 per year, plus an estimated $185,000 or more in recruiting fees and onboarding costs. The systems Mr. Forbes built from scratch represent institutional knowledge that cannot be easily transferred.

Recommended Action

Based on this analysis, a market-aligned compensation package for Mr. Forbes's documented skill set, scope, and output would fall in the range of $240,000–$280,000 in total compensation, accompanied by a title adjustment to Vice President, Technology & Innovation or Chief Technology Officer. This range reflects the blended value of his six documented skill domains, the financial services industry premium, and his demonstrated strategic impact on the firm.
